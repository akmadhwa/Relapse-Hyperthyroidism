Treatment Options

There are three types of treatment options for hyperthyroidism relapse.

Anti-thyroid drugs

Several treatment options are available if you have hyperthyroidism. The most common treatment in Malaysia is to use anti-thyroid medications to slow the production of thyroid hormones.

Function:
To treat an overactive thyroid by stopping your thyroid from producing excess hormones.

Type of medicines :
Methimazole, Carbimazole and Propylthiouracil (PTU). These medicines are known as thionamides.
Methimazole is usually the first choice of oral medication as PTU carries a higher (though still low overall) risk of liver damage.

Dosage/Duration:
You will need to take the medicine daily for a month or two. The treatment continues for about one to two years and the dose is slowly reduced until it is finally stopped.

Radioactive Iodine

Radioactive iodine treatment is where radiation is used to damage your thyroid, in order to limit the amount of hormones it can produce.

Treatment:
Radiation is used to damage your thyroid, in order to limit the amount of hormones produced. You're given a drink or capsule that contains a low dose of radiation, which is then absorbed by your thyroid. Most people only require a single treatment.


Duration:
It can take a few weeks or months for the full benefits to be felt, so you may need to take one of the anti-thyroid medications orally for a short time.

Do not consider this!
If you are pregnant, breastfeeding, have confirmed or suspected thyroid cancer, and if you are unable to follow radiation safety guidelines and for females planning a pregnancy within 4-6 months.

Thyroid surgery (Thyroidectomy)

Treatment: Surgery
You can remove part or all of the thyroid gland. Removing the entire thyroid gland is normally recommended, as this means there's no chance of the symptoms coming back.
